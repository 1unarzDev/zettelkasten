### Verbs
- There are plural and singular verbs. It is easiest to identify which is which by prefixing the verb with a pronoun such as "they" for plural and "she" for singular
- There are 12 different verb tenses in English made by combining timeframes (past, present, and future) with aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous)  
	- Simple: expresses actions without indicating whether it is ongoing or completed
	- Continuous: emphasizes ongoing, unfinished actions often using the "-ing" suffix
	- Perfect: indicates actions that have already been completed in relation to a specific time
	- Perfect Continuous: show ongoing actions that began in the past or continue up to another point in time

|                    | Past                               | Present                             | Future                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Simple             | I *walked* to the store.           | I *walk* to the store.              | I *will walk* to the store.              |
| Continuous         | I *was walking* to the store.      | I *am walking* to the store.        | I *will be walking* to the store.        |
| Perfect            | I *had walked* to the store.       | I *have walked* to the store.       | I *will have walked* to the store.       |
| Perfect Continuous | I *had been walking* to the store. | I *have been walking* to the store. | I *will have been walking* to the store. |

### Apostrophes
- Singular
	- Normal: Juliana's cat is mean.
	- Weird: James's cat is very nice. / James' cat is very nice.
- Plural
	- Normal: The cats' tails are so fluffy.
	- Weird: The children's cats have no right to eat my dinnner.
- Plural Individuals
	- Individual Possession: Maria's and Omar's cats are very beautiful
	- Shared Possession: Robert and Natalie's cat is ugly.
