- **One-time purchase apps**: Very weak for drawing in newer customers; only very effective with a large, established brand where many people are recommending the product already
- **Subscription-based**: Subscription models are good for stable revenue because even if downloads aren't as high for a given month, you still have cash flow from a different source. [A/B Testing](https://unbounce.com/a-b-testing/ab-testing-pricing/) involves collecting data about different ways of displaying your subscription to make them more attractive for users. It is also possible to test different prices, but there are also ethical considerations. Pricing differently in each region can also be used to help boost income.
- **In-app purchases**: Additional desirable or useful features. Most common in games or productivity apps.
- **Advertisements, Affiliate, or Referral Revenue**: Best for apps with high daily active users (generally some form of entertainment). You can utilize something known as the waterfall method where different ad platforms bid for the highest payment. 
- **Data licensing**: Selling user data or insights under strict laws
