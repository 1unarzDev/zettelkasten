### Routine
- 30-45 minutes targeted practice daily
- Logging missed questions with explanation on the correct solving approach and justification (research as needed)
- Practice under timed conditions (practice tests) occasionally to get pacing correct and see progress

### Strategies

#### General
- All the information you need to answer questions can be found in the passage, so avoid letting your previous knowledge make you uncertain about the correct answer choice; instead objectively justify and eliminate answer choices by using direct text evidence
- After reading the question and getting an understanding of what it's asking, lightly annotate key points in the text to absorb information without rereading and to stay engaged
- Avoid thinking about how correct an answer is, and instead finding the things wrong with answer choices
	- Does it deviate away from the point being asked?
	- Does it talk about a part of the text irrelevant to the question?
- Correct answer choices shouldn't contain irrelevant components to the passage or question
- Avoid answer choices with extreme vocabulary unless there is a direct connection to what the passage says, these words will often make the choice incorrect
- For the questions using the notes to support the relevant claim, you can skip reading the notes entirely and just use the answer choices because they will always use information found in the notes
- Questions asking about the structure of text are always looking for shifts in the passage
- When a question asks, "what finding, if true, would support the claim," you can generally skip to the end where the claim is, identifying the answer choice that supports it
- For transition questions, I think it is most logical to highlight the point before the transition and the point after the transition, focusing on how their messages relate (are they contrasting or related) 

#### Vocab
- Since it is impossible to know every difficult word the SAT may throw at you, the way many questions are designed is to use your understanding of prefixes, suffixes, and roots to deduce the correct answer
- Sometimes you will be given three positive/negative terms with one that stands out from the others, using the context of the passage can allow you to determine the correct answer (focus on the type of word that fits will in the blank)
- For vocab, come up with a synonym of the answer using direct text evidence choice to match the answer choices with it (flip the thing that is happening and what it is happening to)

### [[SAT Grammar Mechanics|Grammar Rules]]
- Avoid reading nonessential information for punctuation questions
- Use "they" and "she" to find the odd answer out by combining it with verbs to identify which one is plural or singular unlike the other answer choices
- If that trick doesn't work, then you are dealing with a verb tenses question (read through and identify the tense from another part of the sentence and match it with the answer choice)
- Eliminate information between commas and em dashes to speed up your reading process
- Modifier questions you can flip the blank after the comma to the front of the sentence and see if it makes sense still
	- The thing at the beginning of the blank should be what the action is happening to, so if the question is "Upon recovering two years later, `______` forcing an angered Richard from the royal court and precipitating a series of battles later known as the War of Roses." The answer choice shouldn't begin with "the reign," "Henry's reign," or "it was Henry," and instead something like "Henry resumed his reign."

### Studying
- Since a lot of grammar rules textbooks or guides are likely to contain excess basic things I already understand, using a highly compact list of rules to know and keeping note of any questions I was hesitant about should ensure I get every grammar question correct
- SAT reading rules and tips can be gathered by writing some basic notes while riding in the car doing nothing
- Take word roots seriously: repeatedly quiz yourself on them using any convenient tool
- Frequently do common SAT vocab Quizlets (but don't forget that it is more important to develop the skill of guessing)
